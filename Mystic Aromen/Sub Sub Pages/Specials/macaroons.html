Recipe Hello there, Bold Bakers. So what are we waiting for, let's just get on with it.
Prep: 30min         Cook:20min
Total: 2hr         Yield: Approx 30 macaroons

1.   2cups powdered sugar (200 g)
In the bowl of a food processor, combine the powdered sugar, almond flour, and ½ teaspoon of salt, and process on low speed, until extra fine.

2.   Half a cup of granulated sugar (50 g)
Gradually add the granulated sugar until fully incorporated. Continue to beat until stiff peaks form

3.   1 cup almond flour (95 g), finely ground
After the last addition of almond flour, continue to fold slowly until the batter falls into ribbons and you can make a figure 8 while holding the spatula up.

4.   Half a teaspoon vanilla extract
Add the vanilla and beat until incorporated. Add the food coloring and beat until just combined.

5.   1 teaspoon salt, divided
Place 4 dots of the batter in each corner of a rimmed baking sheet, and place a piece of parchment paper over it, using the batter to help adhere the parchment to the baking sheet.

6.   2 drops pink gel food coloring
Tap the baking sheet on a flat surface 5 times to release any air bubbles. Let the macarons sit at room temperature for 30 minutes to 1 hour, until dry to the touch.

7.   3 egg whites, at room temperature
Bake the macarons for 17 minutes, until the feet are well-risen and the macarons don’t stick to the parchment paper.

8.   2 sticks, at room temperature
Let the macarons sit at room temperature for 30 minutes to 1 hour, until dry to the touch. Preheat the oven to 300˚F (150˚C).

9.   1 cup unsalted butter (230 g)
Make the buttercream: In a large bowl, add the butter and beat with a mixer for 1 minute until light and fluffy. Sift in the powdered sugar and beat until fully incorporated.

10.   3 tablespoons heavy cream
ransfer the buttercream to a piping bag fitted with a round tip. Add a dollop of buttercream to one macaron shell. Top it with another macaron shell to create a sandwich. Repeat with remaining.

11.   3 egg whites, at room temperature
Bake the macarons for 17 minutes, until the feet are well-risen and the macarons don’t stick to the parchment paper.
